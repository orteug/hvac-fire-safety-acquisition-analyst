# Deal Screening Criteria: HVAC & Fire Safety SMBs

> **Last Updated:** May 2026
> **Refresh Cadence:** Review annually or when valuation multiples in `reference/valuation-methodology.md` are updated — thresholds are calibrated to current market conditions.

This reference document defines the evaluation criteria used in every deal screen. It is organized by category and includes pass/caution/flag thresholds based on micro-PE acquisition standards for operator-buyer deals in the $500K–$5M revenue range.

---

## 1. Financial Health

| Metric | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| SDE/EBITDA Margin | >20% | 12–20% | <12% |
| Revenue Trend (3yr) | Growing or stable | Flat ±5% | Declining >10% YOY |
| Revenue Concentration (top client) | <15% | 15–30% | >30% |
| Revenue Concentration (top 3 clients) | <30% | 30–50% | >50% |
| Asking Multiple (on SDE) | <3.5x | 3.5–4.5x | >4.5x |
| Revenue Multiple | <0.6x | 0.6–1.0x | >1.0x |

**Notes:**
- SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) = Net income + owner's salary/benefits + non-cash expenses + one-time expenses
- Always verify SDE via 3-year P&L, not seller's self-report
- Revenue concentration in Fire Safety is more tolerable if accounts are under long-term inspection contracts

---

## 2. Recurring Revenue Quality

| Metric | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| Service/maintenance agreement count | >200 for HVAC, >50 commercial for F&LS | 50–200 | <50 |
| % of revenue from recurring sources | >35% | 15–35% | <15% |
| Agreement auto-renewal rate | >80% | 60–80% | <60% |
| Install vs. service revenue split | <60% install | 60–75% install | >75% install |

**Notes:**
- High install revenue is a leading indicator of revenue volatility. Installs are one-time; service is recurring.
- Fire & Life Safety inspection contracts are inherently more sticky (compliance-driven) than HVAC maintenance agreements. Weight accordingly.
- Ask to see the actual service agreement roster, not just a count. Verify active status and last service date.

---

## 3. Owner-Operator Dependency

This is the #1 failure mode in trades acquisitions. Evaluate with brutal honesty.

| Area | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|------|-------|---------|----------|
| Owner's operational role | Strategic/admin only | Partial field work | Primary tech or only salesperson |
| Client relationships | Held by team or systems | Mixed | All relationships flow through owner |
| Institutional knowledge | Documented SOPs exist | Partially documented | All in owner's head |
| Transition willingness | 12+ month overlap | 6–12 months | <6 months or unwilling |

**Key questions to ask:**
1. If the owner took a 30-day vacation today, what breaks?
2. Which clients would leave if the owner left?
3. Who is the owner's #2 — and could they run daily ops for 90 days?

---

## 4. Team & Human Capital

| Metric | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| Total certified technicians | 4+ | 2–3 | 1 (owner only) |
| Lead technician / field supervisor exists | Yes | Informal | No |
| Average tech tenure | >3 years | 1–3 years | <1 year or high turnover |
| Non-compete or key-person agreements | In place | Partial | None |

**Notes:**
- For Fire & Life Safety, verify NICET certification levels. NICET II+ for inspectors is minimum credibility.
- For HVAC, verify EPA 608 certification and any state-specific HVAC contractor licensing.
- High tech turnover in a trades business is a structural problem, not a staffing problem. Dig into why.

---

## 5. Geographic & Market Position

| Factor | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| Market position | Dominant local player or top 3 | Known but not dominant | Undifferentiated competitor |
| Service radius | Compact, high-density | Moderate | Sprawling, thin coverage |
| Population trend (market) | Growing | Stable | Declining |
| Competitive landscape | Fragmented (many small players) | Mixed | One dominant regional chain |

---

## 6. Asset Condition

| Asset | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|-------|-------|---------|----------|
| Fleet age | <5 years average | 5–8 years | >8 years or deferred maintenance |
| Equipment owned vs. leased | Owned or favorable leases | Mix | Operating leases with escalators |
| Real property | Leased with favorable terms | Month-to-month | Owner-occupied (lease-to-close complexity) |
| Tools/inventory | Current, well-maintained | Aging | Outdated or missing for scope of work |

---

## 7. Legal & Compliance

Any of the following = automatic deeper review before proceeding:

- Active litigation (vendor, customer, employee)
- EPA refrigerant violations or unpermitted installations
- AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction) citations for Fire Safety work
- Unlicensed contractor history
- Workers' comp claims patterns suggesting unsafe job sites
- Customer complaints pattern on BBB, Google, or contractor licensing boards
