## Recurring Revenue (F&LS-Specific Thresholds)

| Metric | Green | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| Active inspection contracts (commercial) | >50 | 20–50 | <20 |
| % of revenue from inspection/monitoring | >50% | 30–50% | <30% |
| Monitoring MRR | >$10K/mo | $3K–$10K/mo | <$3K/mo |
| Contract average length | 3–5 year | 1–2 year | Month-to-month |

Critical nuance: F&LS inspection revenue is compliance-driven — clients do not cancel because of price or preference. They cancel because of service failure or relationship breakdown. This stickiness is the core acquisition thesis for F&LS deals.

## Monitoring Book — Standalone Valuation

Any F&LS deal with $5K+ monthly MRR from monitoring:

**Monitoring book valuation framework:**
- Industry standard: 30–45× monthly MRR for residential/small commercial monitoring
- Strategic buyer premium: up to 50× MRR when book integrates into existing platform infrastructure
- At $5K MRR: standalone book value $150K–$250K
- At $15K MRR: standalone book value $450K–$750K

Assess separately from full-business valuation. A deal that fails as a full acquisition may contain a monitoring book worth absorbing by:
- Pye-Barker Fire & Safety
- Sciens Building Solutions
- Regional central station operators

Always include this assessment even on PASS verdicts.

## Inspection Contract Stickiness

F&LS inspection revenue is legally mandated — the stickiness comes from compliance obligation, not customer loyalty. But it can still be disrupted:

Green indicators:
- Contracts are with the building owner/management company (not individual contacts)
- Multi-year contracts with auto-renewal
- AHJ relationships documented in business records, not just the owner's head

Red flags:
- Contracts are personal relationships (follow the owner, not the entity)
- Contracts expired but service continues informally
- Owner is the AHJ contact of record

## NICET Certification Requirements

| Level | Required for |
|-------|-------------|
| NICET II | Minimum for inspection credibility in commercial market |
| NICET III | Required for supervising complex systems (suppression, special hazard) |
| NICET IV | Required for engineering review and plan approval |

Verify NICET levels for every inspector. An inspection business with only NICET I techs cannot service complex commercial clients.

For suppression work: verify NFPA 25 and relevant state fire code compliance training.

## AHJ Relationships — Moat Assessment

Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) relationships are a real moat in F&LS:
- AHJ relationships take years to build
- A good AHJ relationship can mean faster permit approvals, advance notice of code changes, less friction on inspections
- These relationships live with the owner, not the entity — assess transferability

Screen questions:
1. Who handles AHJ communications? Owner, office staff, or field supervisors?
2. How many AHJs does the business interact with regularly?
3. Have any AHJ contacts been introduced to a potential successor?

## F&LS Regulatory Compliance — Specific Flags

Automatic deeper review triggers (beyond shared criteria):
- AHJ citations for substandard inspection work
- ITM (Inspection, Testing, and Maintenance) records missing or incomplete
- Deficiency notifications not followed up — creates liability if a system fails
- Unlicensed installation work in prior years
- Suppression agent handling violations

## F&LS-Specific Asking Multiple Context

Standard multiples (when recurring compliance revenue base is strong):
- Inspection-dominant business: up to 5.0× SDE is defensible (compliance stickiness justifies premium)
- Monitoring-dominant business: can approach 5.5× if MRR base is clean and contractual
- Installation-only business: treat as HVAC — 3.5× SDE ceiling without recurring base

If asking >4.5×: require explicit accounting for ITM recurring revenue, monitoring MRR, and contract term length before accepting the multiple.
