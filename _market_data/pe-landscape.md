# PE Landscape: HVAC & Fire Safety Acquisitions

## Why This Matters for Operator-Buyers

Private equity has been aggressively consolidating HVAC and Fire & Life Safety businesses since 2018. In 2023–2025, deal velocity accelerated: platforms are now competing for businesses with as little as $750K–$1M EBITDA, and some are targeting sub-$500K EBITDA tuck-ins when geography or customer concentration is attractive.

**The implication:** If a deal looks good to you, it probably looks good to a platform. Move faster than you think you need to.

---

## Active PE Platforms — HVAC

### Apex Service Partners
- **Backed by:** Apax Partners
- **Strategy:** Aggressive residential/commercial HVAC roll-up; 50+ platforms nationally
- **Target profile:** $1M–$5M revenue, >$200K EBITDA, Sun Belt or high-growth markets preferred
- **Acquisition approach:** Proprietary outreach to owners; fast LOI (often within 2–3 weeks of first call)
- **Multiple range:** 4.5–6x EBITDA for clean, recurring-revenue businesses
- **Operator-buyer edge:** Apex closes fast but owners who want legacy preservation often prefer a local operator

### Authority Brands (HVAC segment)
- **Strategy:** Franchise + acquisition hybrid; targets service businesses with strong local brand
- **Target profile:** $2M–$8M revenue; conversion to franchise model post-acquisition
- **Note:** This is not a clean tuck-in; ownership structure changes significantly

### Service Titan-backed operator networks
- **Emerging:** Groups of operators co-acquiring and sharing back-office via ServiceTitan + shared CFO
- **Not PE in the traditional sense but competitive for deals in the $500K–$2M range**

## Active PE Platforms — Fire & Life Safety

### Pye-Barker Fire & Safety
- **Backed by:** Thomas H. Lee Partners
- **Strategy:** National F&LS roll-up; one of the most aggressive acquirers in the space
- **Target profile:** $1M–$20M revenue; inspection-heavy, multi-service F&LS businesses
- **Acquisition approach:** Direct owner outreach, broker relationships; very fast process
- **Multiple range:** 5–7x EBITDA for well-contracted inspection businesses
- **Operator-buyer edge:** Pye-Barker wins on price. Compete on seller relationship and speed to LOI.
- **Monitoring book acquisitions:** Pye-Barker will acquire standalone monitoring contract portfolios as strategic add-ons to existing local operations — even when the parent business doesn't meet full tuck-in criteria. If a deal has $5K+ MRR with clean contracts, the monitoring book may have a buyer independent of the overall business quality.

### Sciens Building Solutions
- **Backed by:** Investcorp
- **Strategy:** Technology-forward F&LS platform; emphasizes monitoring + inspection integration
- **Target profile:** $2M+ revenue, commercial accounts, technology-forward operations
- **Multiple range:** 5–6.5x EBITDA
- **Note:** Sciens is selective — smaller or more manual businesses are less likely to attract them
- **Monitoring book acquisitions:** Sciens actively targets monitoring contract portfolios in their Northeast and Florida markets as density plays. A standalone monitoring book with $5K+ MRR in a Sciens-active geography is a strategic acquisition candidate regardless of the parent business's overall profile.

### APi Group (NYSE: APG)
- **Public company roll-up:** Specialty contracting, including F&LS
- **Strategy:** Large-scale acquisitions; targets $5M+ revenue platforms
- **Operator-buyer relevance:** Low — APi is not competing for micro-deals under $3M revenue

### Altus Fire & Life Safety
- **Regional platform:** Southeast and Mid-Atlantic focus
- **Target profile:** $1M–$5M revenue, inspection + suppression, commercial accounts
- **Multiple range:** 4.5–5.5x EBITDA

## PE Acquisition Criteria — At a Glance

| Criterion | PE Minimum (tuck-in) | Operator-Buyer Range |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| EBITDA | $300K–$500K+ | $150K–$500K |
| Revenue | $1M+ | $500K+ |

| Geography | Growth markets preferred | Any defensible local market |
| Owner-dependency | Will accept with right structure | Higher risk tolerance with earnout |
| Multiple paid | 4.5–7x EBITDA | 2.5–5x SDE/EBITDA |

## How an Operator-Buyer Competes Against PE

### PE's Advantages
- Higher headline multiple (1–2 turns more than operator-buyer)
- Fast, professional process with dedicated deal teams
- Seller gets liquidity AND ongoing upside via equity roll (rollover equity)
- Known brand name can help with bank financing

### Operator-Buyer's Advantages
- **Legacy preservation:** Many owners do NOT want their company absorbed into a national brand
- **Relationship continuity:** Employees, customers, and vendors often prefer local ownership
- **Speed and certainty:** A prepared operator-buyer with SBA pre-approval can close faster than a platform requiring board approval
- **Flexibility on deal structure:** Operator-buyers can offer more creative earnouts, consulting arrangements, and transition support that PE standardizes away
- **No earn-out complexity:** PE rollover equity is illiquid; a clean all-cash or SBA deal is simpler

### Competitive Tactics for Operator-Buyers

1. **Move fast.** If the business profile (EBITDA >$300K, recurring revenue, clean team) makes it PE-attractive, assume you have 4–8 weeks before a platform makes contact.

2. **Get SBA pre-approval before going to LOI.** Sellers and brokers take you more seriously. Eliminates a major closing risk.

3. **Lead with relationship, not multiple.** Ask about the owner's goals for the employees, customers, and legacy. PE can't credibly promise legacy preservation.

4. **Offer a seller note with upside participation.** A performance-based seller note that pays more if revenue holds is often more motivating than a slightly higher PE rollover.

5. **Go direct when possible.** Many owners in this segment are not working with brokers yet. A warm introduction or direct outreach letter beats a competitive auction process.

## Signals That PE Has Already Contacted the Seller

- Seller mentions "several groups have reached out"
- Seller is using M&A-specific language (LOI, EBITDA multiples, rollover equity) unprompted
- Broker is running a formal process with a data room
- Asking price is at or above 5x SDE (seller has been coached on PE-level pricing)

**If any of these are true:** Accelerate. Issue LOI with exclusivity clause within 2 weeks or assume you're in a competitive process.

## Deals Below PE Radar

These profiles are typically below PE acquisition threshold — operator-buyer has clear field:

- Sub-$300K EBITDA with heavy owner-dependency
- Single-inspector F&LS business with no team depth
- Revenue <$750K with no clear path to $2M+
- Geographic markets: rural or low-density metros (PE prefers top 50 MSAs)
- Install-heavy with no recurring base

**Note:** "Below PE radar" does NOT mean the deal is good. It means there is no competitive pressure — use the time to negotiate structure carefully.
